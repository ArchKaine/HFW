Select a ship family or a unique capital-class vessel to view its specialized variants developed by Hephaestus Forge Works. Each variant is tailored for specific roles within HFW's covert operations doctrine.

The Anvil Spectre project introduces a new echelon of covert warfare vessels. The operational doctrine emphasizes the coordinated deployment of the Spectre family, allowing them to tackle complex threats through combined arms tactics.

Spectre Shadow (Stealth/Electronic Warfare)

Role: The premier stealth platform and information warfare hub. Designed for deep infiltration, intelligence gathering, data hacking, and providing an electronic warfare umbrella for other HFW assets.

Description: The Shadow is HFW's truest "ghost." It is designed for persistent, long-duration operations deep within hostile territory. It fights not with overwhelming firepower, but with data, deception, and darkness, turning an enemy's own sensor network against them.

Key Features & Systems:

  • Pinnacle Stealth Suite: Features the exclusive Adaptive Countershading system, a sophisticated EW package running on a dedicated Size 2 computer blade, and enhanced acoustic dampening.
  • Data Warfare: Equipped with advanced data-interception tools and remote hacking transmitters.
  • Thermal Obscurant Launchers: Modified turret emplacements deploy "hot smoke," creating a massive thermal bloom to decoy heat-seeking ordnance and break sensor locks.
  • Armament: 2x forward-firing Size 5 Railguns for self-defense or opportunistic strikes.

Spectre Strike (Heavy Attack & Ground Support)

Role: A dedicated heavy strike platform that uses stealth to achieve the perfect attack vector before delivering a devastating, overwhelming blow.

Description: The Strike embodies the principle of decisive violence from an unexpected direction. Its stealth systems, while less persistent than the Shadow's, are sufficient to get it into position. Once it opens fire, its objective is total annihilation of the target.

Key Features & Systems:

  • Overwhelming Firepower: A formidable forward-facing battery of 2x Size 5 and 2x Size 4 Railguns, capable of using specialized HFW Penetrator Munitions.
  • Railgun Overcharge: Experimental capacitors allow for a single, terrifyingly powerful shot that generates immense heat and a massive signature bloom—a "finishing move."
  • Ground Attack Specialist: Equipped with more numerous, fast-tracking Incendiary Turrets for saturating ground targets.

Spectre Lockdown (Quantum Interdiction)

Role: The gatekeeper of the battlefield. The Lockdown's purpose is to manipulate quantum travel, trapping key targets, preventing enemy fleets from escaping, and cutting off reinforcements.

Description: By controlling the flow of battle on a strategic level, the Lockdown creates an inescapable cage in the middle of empty space. It uses its stealth to covertly position itself at a key jump point, waiting for the perfect moment to spring its trap.

Key Features & Systems:

  • Quantum Enforcement Device (QED): Its core is a powerful, long-range Size 3 QED capable of generating a quantum snare large and strong enough to pull multiple ships from quantum travel.
  • Stealth Deployment: Its signature reduction is optimized to mask the massive energy build-up of the QED right up until the moment of activation.
  • Armament: 1x QED (Size 3), supported by 2x forward-firing Size 5 Railguns to engage snared targets.

Spectre Pulse (Systems Disablement)

Role: HFW's non-lethal solution. The Pulse is designed to disable, disrupt, and neutralize enemy vessels without destroying them, making it the ideal tool for capture, boarding actions, and asset denial.

Description: The Pulse is a master of electronic and energy warfare, but where the Shadow is subtle, the Pulse is a brute. It overwhelms enemy systems with raw power, leaving them dead in the water.

Key Features & Systems:

  • EMP Warfare: Built around a powerful, wide-arc Size 3 EMP generator capable of being "focused" for greater range.
  • Distortion & Torpedoes: Features 2x Size 5 Distortion Cannons to strip shields, supported by four integrated launch tubes carrying a payload of 24x Size 5 EMP Torpedoes.
  • Lethal Backup: For targets that must be destroyed, it retains 2x Size 4 Railguns.

Spectre Revenant (Heavy Interceptor/Light Destroyer)

Classification: Light Destroyer / Heavy Interceptor

Manufacturer: Hephaestus Forge Works (XL Shipyard Division, The Forge)

Status: Highly classified, limited production, dedicated HFW asset.

I. Design Philosophy & Role

The Spectre Revenant project was initiated by HFW as a parallel, even more highly classified effort than the standard Spectre program. It was conceived with a singular purpose: to serve as the ultimate sentinel for HFW's most valuable asset, The Forge. While the other Spectre variants are "scalpels" designed for surgical strikes, the Revenant is HFW's shield and hammer—a purpose-built defensive dreadnought intended to neutralize any significant threat to their hidden sanctuary, up to and including capital ships.

It leverages the core technological advancements of the Spectre project but scales them to a massive 121-meter frame, prioritizing direct combat effectiveness and survivability. Its very existence represents HFW's triumph in achieving Shadow-class stealth on a platform of unprecedented size, allowing it to dictate the terms of any defensive engagement.

II. General Specifications & Key Features

  • Dimensions: Length: 121m | Beam: 84+m | Height: 20m (23m gear down)
  • Crew Complement: 3–5 (average 3), enabled by advanced HFW automation.
  • Cargo Capacity: ~270 SCU, capable of carrying up to three Ursa rovers.
  • Dual Combat Doctrine: Engineered for both long-range precision sniping with its heavy forward armament and overwhelming close-quarters "knife fights," using its stealth to close distance.
  • Capital Threat Neutralization: Explicitly designed and armed to effectively engage and neutralize frigate- and destroyer-class vessels.
  • HFW "Aegis Protocol" System Hardening: Exceptionally resilient to EMP, distortion, and other energy-based warfare.

IV. The Phoenix Protocol (Pilot Training Program)

Operating the Spectre Revenant requires a unique blend of tactical intuition and unparalleled resilience. To cultivate the elite individuals capable of mastering the Revenant, HFW initiated the **"Phoenix Protocol"**. This is a radical fusion of direct neural interface technology and a state-of-the-art, highly classified regeneration facility housed deep within The Forge.

  • The Process: Pilots interface directly with simulations that are indistinguishable from reality. A tactical error resulting in simulated "death" triggers the regeneration facility via the neural interface, allowing the pilot to rapidly internalize critical lessons from their "fatal" mistakes.
  • HFW's Rationale: This ethically complex program is deemed necessary due to the Revenant's immense cost and strategic importance, providing a path to unparalleled mastery in a compressed timeframe.
  • The Legend: Whispers persist within HFW of two pilots—Cmdr. Ghost and Jax Rylan—who, in separate trials, completed the entire qualification gauntlet without a single simulated death.

V. Acquisition & Deployment Doctrine

The Spectre Revenant cannot be purchased. It is an undertaking tied directly to a player's actions and reputation. It can only be acquired through two paths: **Lawful Acquisition** (recovering a lost Revenant for HFW, who then "gifts" it to the player) or **Criminal Acquisition** (stealing one directly from HFW). In either case, the ship becomes account-bound and cannot be traded. If a player betrays HFW's trust or continues to act against them, the loss of their Revenant is permanent, and they receive the **"Mark of HFW,"** preventing them from ever acquiring another.

The Anvil Eidolon Series: Agile Shadows for Close Support

Following the successful deployment and operational validation of the Spectre series, Hephaestus Forge Works recognized a critical gap in its "shadow fleet." While Spectres excelled at deep infiltration and strategic strikes, they lacked the agility required for close-in escort, rapid interception, and localized point defense.

The Anvil Valkyrie Phantom: Stealthy Insertion

Recognizing that even the most potent strike or disabling force requires boots on the ground to secure an objective, Hephaestus Forge Works developed the Anvil Valkyrie Phantom, a specialized variant of Anvil's robust Valkyrie dropship. HFW's approach was to adopt the proven airframe, strip it of all non-essential or signature-heavy components, and then perform all subsequent R&D, modification, and production internally at The Forge to create a dropship capable of operating alongside the Spectre series.

Valkyrie Phantom (Base)

Role: Stealth Assault Dropship & Gunship

Description: The standard Phantom is engineered to deliver a full contingent of 20 operators and an Ursa-class vehicle into contested territory with minimal detection. It retains the Valkyrie's inherent toughness and a significant armament, allowing it to provide fire support upon arrival and survive extraction if stealth is compromised.

Key Features & Systems:

  • HFW Stealth Retrofit: A frame re-engineered with multi-layered composites, geometric shaping, and active masking systems to drastically reduce its signature.
  • Acoustically Dampened Propulsion: Heavily modified VTOL and main thrusters for quiet, low-altitude insertions.
  • Fully Retractable Armament: All primary weapon systems are housed in HFW-designed retractable mounts or stealth pods.
  • 'Ghost Mode' Turret Stations: Remote turret stations are integrated with Spectre 'ghost mode' display technology, providing gunners with high-fidelity, panoramic views from the safety of the main hull.
  • HFW "Aegis Protocol" System Hardening: Standard on all HFW craft, providing exceptional resilience to EMP and distortion damage.

Armament:

  • Pilot Fixed Guns: 2x Size 2 Hardpoints (Retractable Housings)
  • Remote Wing Turrets: 2x Size 5 Remote Turrets (each with Size 4 repeaters)
  • Retractable Remote Turrets (Top/Bottom): 2x Size 4 Remote Turrets (each with 2x Size 3 guns)
  • Door Guns: 2x Size 1 Gimbal Mounts

Valkyrie Phantom Pathfinder

Role: Stealthy Reconnaissance and Forward Observation.

Description: This variant is designed to precede the main insertion force, providing clandestine "eyes on target." It's tasked with gathering critical, real-time intelligence on enemy positions, patrol routes, and potential ambushes, ensuring the main force has a safe vector for approach.

Key Modifications:

  • Enhanced Sensor Suite: A portion of the troop bay is reconfigured to house an advanced, long-range sensor array and a dedicated signals intelligence (SIGINT) station.
  • Expanded Drone Capacity: Features enhanced deployment and monitoring capabilities for a larger complement of reconnaissance and EW drones.
  • Reduced Transport: Troop capacity is often reduced to accommodate the extra equipment and its operators, typically carrying a small scout team rather than a full assault force.

Valkyrie Phantom Breacher

Role: Covert Entry and Assault Facilitation.

Description: The Breacher is a specialized tool for creating an entrance where none exists. It is tailored for bypassing hardened defenses and cracking fortified structures to clear a path for the main assault teams.

Key Modifications:

  • Breaching Equipment Bay: The vehicle bay is reconfigured to carry specialized breaching tools, including heavy-duty plasma torches, seismic charges, and robotic demolition systems.
  • Automated Defense Suppression: Often equipped with automated point-defense turrets near the exit ramps to provide covering fire during a breach.
  • Reinforced Forward Hull: The Phantom's nose and forward sections feature additional armor plating to withstand potential blowback from its own breaching charges. It is designed to work in close coordination with Spectre Strike variants.

Valkyrie Phantom Medical

Role: Combat Search and Rescue (CSAR) and Medical Extraction.

Description: This variant prioritizes casualty collection and field medical care. It utilizes its stealth to approach "hot" landing zones and extract wounded personnel with a reduced risk of detection, turning the tide of a ground engagement by ensuring soldiers can be safely recovered and treated.

Key Modifications:

  • Mobile Trauma Bay: A significant portion of the troop compartment is converted into an advanced mobile medical bay, featuring two Tier 3 medical beds and multiple stasis pods.
  • Specialized Extraction Equipment: Often equipped with a rescue hoist and grappling systems to extract personnel from difficult or dangerous terrain where landing is not possible.
  • Enhanced Shielding: Power is often diverted to enhance shield strength and recharge rates, prioritizing the survival of the craft and its precious cargo over offensive capability.

HFW Daemon Project (Racing Prototype)

Classification: Experimental Racing & Super-Scout Vessel

Manufacturer: Hephaestus Forge Works (Apex Division, The Forge)

Status: Limited production runs sold under strict contract to UEE military forces and approved private pilots.

I. Design Philosophy & Role

The Daemon is HFW's "public" face of performance engineering. Born from an internal challenge inspired by Murray Cup analysis, the project's goal was to push HFW's expertise in propulsion and low-signature materials into a dedicated speed platform. The result is arguably the fastest and most agile single-seat craft in the UEE. While marketed as a high-end, exclusive racer, its military applications as a "super-scout" for rapid reconnaissance are its primary, if unstated, purpose.

II. General Specifications & Key Features

  • Dimensions: Length: 25 meters | Beam (Width): 11.9 meters | Height: 3.69 meters
  • Crew: 1 (Pilot)
  • Cargo: 2 SCU (Accessory bay for tools, spares, or race-day equipment)
  • Propulsion & Control: Features four Size 1 vectored main thrusters and eight omni-thrusters governed by dual redundant flight-assist AIs, allowing for unparalleled 6-DOF control.
  • Signature Management: While not a true stealth ship, its hull is composed of naturally low-EM composite panels and passive IR diffusion meshes, giving it a significantly lower signature than any other ship in its performance class.
  • HFW "Aegis Protocol" System Hardening: Like all HFW vessels, the Daemon's core systems are exceptionally resilient to EMP and distortion damage.
  • Uncompromised Safety: The design has no weapon mounts. It includes a mandatory HFW flight training program for all new pilots and an advanced high-G, fully-enclosed ejection pod.

V. Lore & Operational Doctrine Notes

The Daemon project was initially an internal passion project led by Dr. Thorne and the Ghost Teams to see if HFW's technology could dominate the racing world. The resulting prototype was so effective that it was immediately adapted for a military "super-scout" role. The limited production run is offered to civilian racers under strict, invitation-only contracts, a move that serves two purposes: it allows HFW to publicly test its high-performance propulsion systems in the competitive racing environment, and the exclusivity of the ship adds to the HFW mystique.

HFW Atlas-class Strategic Hauler

Classification: Frigate-class / Strategic Heavy Freighter

Manufacturer: Hephaestus Forge Works (XL Shipyard Division, The Forge)

In Service: Primary HFW logistics platform since 2952

I. Design Philosophy & Role

The HFW Atlas was born from a critical logistical necessity recognized by Dr. Thorne in the late 2940s: as HFW's operations expanded, the need to move vast quantities of sensitive materials, prototype components, and specialized personnel between The Forge and deep-space operational zones became a significant vulnerability. Standard commercial haulers were too slow, too fragile, and left a data trail a blind man could follow.

The Atlas was engineered to solve this problem with a "Strategic Blockade Runner" philosophy. Its purpose is not just to haul cargo, but to ensure that high-value assets arrive at their destination securely and with minimal traceability. As HFW's first frigate-class logistics platform, its primary defenses are its exceptional speed for its size and a suite of signature-reducing technologies that make it a ghost compared to any other freighter in its class. While not a true "stealth ship" like the Spectre or Erebus, the Atlas is designed to be exceptionally difficult to detect at long range and even harder to track, making it the perfect workhorse for an organization that thrives in the shadows.

II. Performance Profile

  • Speed: For its immense size, the Atlas is remarkably fast. Its oversized HFW power plants and high-efficiency thrusters give it a top sub-light speed that can outrun most conventional interceptors and pirate craft that would typically prey on ships of this size. Its quantum drive is a top-tier military-grade unit, emphasizing rapid spooling and high cruise velocity to minimize time in transit.
  • Maneuverability: Ponderous, as expected for a 220-meter freighter often laden with thousands of tons of cargo. However, its powerful HFW maneuvering thrusters and advanced inertial dampeners make it surprisingly responsive for its class during docking, atmospheric entry, and low-speed maneuvers. It is not designed for combat agility.

III. General Specifications

  • Length: 220 meters
  • Beam (Width): 50 meters
  • Height (Thickness): 70 meters
  • Crew Complement: 4-6 (Pilot, Co-Pilot/Navigator, Engineer, 2x Turret Gunners, optional: Logistics Officer)
  • Cargo Capacity: 7,500 SCU

IV. Key HFW Design Features & Systems

  • Signature-Reduced Hull Design: The Atlas's unique, vaguely cetacean shape is not purely aesthetic. Its smooth, blended contours and lack of sharp angles are specifically designed to reduce its cross-section and deflect active sensor pings. The hull itself is constructed with layers of signature-dampening composites.
  • HFW "Cerberus" Multi-Stage Thermal Baffling: An advanced cooling architecture that carefully manages heat signatures. Under normal operation, heat is processed through low-emission radiators. For "silent running," heat is instead shunted into temporary, internal heat sinks, allowing the ship to "go cold" for short periods to evade passive thermal detection.
  • HFW "Aegis Protocol" System Hardening: A foundational HFW design principle. The Atlas features multi-layered electromagnetic shielding for all critical components, decentralized and redundant subsystems, and specialized hull materials that actively dissipate disruptive energy fields, making it exceptionally resilient to EMP and distortion attacks.
  • Advanced Cargo Handling: Includes a forward "Gulp" Ramp integrated into the bulbous bow for large vehicles and an automated magnetic cargo grid for rapid loading and organization of the 7,500 SCU hold.
  • Moderate Electronic Warfare Suite: Equipped with a capable HFW "Wraith" EW package, primarily for jamming targeting locks and spoofing ship identification signals.

All weapon systems are fully retractable to maintain a clean signature profile during transit and to protect them from environmental hazards.

  • Remote Turrets:

    • 2x Ventral (Twin Size 5 Laser Repeaters)
    • 1x Rear-facing (Twin Size 4 Ballistic Gatling) for pursuit deterrence.

VII. Specialized Facilities & Interior Layout

The Atlas's interior is designed for efficiency and long-duration functionality, balancing its massive cargo bay with necessary crew amenities.

  • Flight Deck (Bridge): A spacious, multi-crew bridge located towards the bow, featuring advanced holographic displays and excellent forward and ventral visibility.
  • Logistics & Cargo Control: A dedicated station aft of the bridge where the Logistics Officer can oversee the automated cargo grid, monitor inventory, and manage loading/unloading operations.
  • Main Cargo Hold: The cavernous, 7,500 SCU central hold spans the majority of the ship's length, large enough to accommodate multiple ground vehicles (up to Nova Tank size), small utility craft, or entire prefabricated habitat modules for outpost deployment.
  • Engineering Section: Located aft, providing direct access to the power plants, coolers, and main thruster assemblies.
  • Crew Quarters: Spartan but comfortable quarters for a crew of up to six, including a small galley, mess hall, and fresher facilities.

VIII. Lore & Operational Doctrine Notes

Within HFW, the Atlas fleet is known as the "Unseen Backbone." While the Spectres and Pathfinders conduct the high-profile missions, it is the Atlas crews who undertake the equally dangerous task of keeping these forward assets supplied, fueled, and equipped. Atlas pilots are specialists in threat avoidance, using their ship's surprising speed and low signature to outrun or slip past threats rather than engaging them. The Atlas is the only vessel trusted by HFW to transport its most valuable and volatile assets, where the cargo is often more valuable than the ship itself.

HFW Erebus-Prime (Strategic Command Carrier)

Classification: Super-Capital (Flagship)

Manufacturer: Hephaestus Forge Works (Apex Division, The Forge)

In Service: First hull, HFW *Erebus*, commissioned c. 2955

I. Design Philosophy & Role

The HFW Erebus-Prime is not merely a ship; it is a statement of technological supremacy and a doctrine made manifest. Forged in the most secret and advanced depths of The Forge, it represents the absolute zenith of HFW's mastery over signature reduction and sustained covert warfare at a scale previously thought impossible. Its existence is a closely guarded secret, a strategic asset that redefines the nature of power projection.

Its design philosophy is one of absolute covert supremacy and enduring operational autonomy. Where other capitals project power through overwhelming force, the Erebus-Prime projects it through nearly undetectable presence, information dominance, and the capacity to orchestrate system-spanning operations from a position of complete obscurity. Its extraordinarily wide, multi-faceted blended-wing design is a testament to HFW's genius—a form meticulously crafted to cheat known sensor technologies while housing systems and capabilities that rival mobile starbases. This is the vessel from which HFW can touch any corner of the verse, unseen and unheard, until it chooses to reveal its terrifying might.

II. Performance Profile

  • Speed: For its colossal size, its HFW-designed main thrusters and oversized power plants give it a top sub-light speed that is faster than any other known ship of comparable capital size when not in full stealth mode.
  • Maneuverability: While a super-capital behemoth, the Erebus-Prime incorporates HFW's most advanced inertial dampening fields, granting it responsiveness and maneuverability that is unprecedented for a vessel of its immense mass.

III. General Specifications

  • Length: 400 meters
  • Beam (Width): 288 meters
  • Height: 70 meters
  • Crew Complement: 40 Optimal (enabled by advanced HFW automation)
  • Mission Complement: Capacity for up to 160 additional personnel
  • Cargo Capacity: 6,000 SCU

IV. Armament & Defensive Systems

The armament is focused on surgical long-range strikes and overwhelming self-defense if its stealth is compromised. All systems are fully retractable.

  • Primary Spinal Armament (2x HFW "Tartarus Reach" Heavy Plasma Lances): The pinnacle of directed energy weapon technology. The Tartarus Lance projects a sustained, magnetically-contained stream of star-hot plasma, designed for extreme range and surgical precision against capital-class shields and armor.
  • Heavy Defensive Turrets: 4x Twin-linked Size 7 HFW "Cerberus" Heavy Plasma Accelerator Turrets, providing key defensive arcs against cruiser-class threats.
  • Point Defense Network: A dense screen of 16x "Hydra's Fury" Quad-Barrel Laser Emplacements and 8x "Gorgon Eye" Swarm Defense Flak Pods.
  • Strategic Ordnance Delivery System: A massive internal magazine services 12x Size 10 Heavy Torpedo Tubes, allowing the Erebus to carry a devastating payload of 48 capital-grade torpedoes for sustained, long-duration missions.

V. Hangar & Support Craft Complement

The Erebus's primary role as a command carrier is fulfilled by its massive, central hangar bay, which uses the ship's extreme width to house a significant operational force.

  • Stealth Fighter Wing: Up to 16 HFW Eidolon-class multi-role stealth fighters.
  • Support & Assault Craft: Up to 4 HFW Valkyrie Phantom dropships.
  • Reconnaissance & Command Support: Up to 2 HFW Pathfinder MKII deep recon vessels.
  • Drone Fleet: Hundreds of specialized drones for reconnaissance, EW, repair, and logistical support.
